Introduction
Bomix is a new way for users to play music, not with instruments, but with their body. In this new input system, the core idea is that you can control the height of a note by the position of your hand and the rythm of music by your hands' shaking frequency. What' more, you can try different instruments with your legs! Sounds exciting!
In the course of interaction design, we conducted user research with 4 participants, in order to make sure that the interaction of BoMix is intuitive and natural enough for users to perform.
01 Problem Space
For thousands of years, physical instruments have been used to play music. But physical instruments are usually either large in size or inconvenient to be carried with. In addition, it’s particularly difficult for university students to have a regular musical activity because of the limited space of their dorm and because of the noise. Therefore, we propose a new method of playing music, called “BoMix”. In this new input system, the user only need his or her body to make music.
02 Solution
Basic Idea
When users move their arms in different areas in the air, BoMix will play different piano notes.

When users move their legs in the air, BoMix will play drum notes based on users' rhythm.

The mapping rules between different body parts and different types of instruments, different midair areas and different music notes can be customized basaed on users' personal preferences and needs.

Our device is using the Kinect sensor and is based on the metaphore of a musical range.

03 Design Process
04 Research & Synthesis
User Scenarios
Design Iteration
First Stage
There is only a visual display of the user's body on the first iteration of BoMix interface.
Second Stage
Visual indicators were added to the second iteration of BoMix interface, to make the music notes generated by the current gesture. This can help users create more professionally, which adds more practical value to BoMix.
Third Stage
Reference lines were added to facilitate users make a desired gesture more efficiently and more accurately. Such design makes the interface more visible and easier to interact with.
